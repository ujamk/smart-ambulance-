# 📅 Meeting Notes – Smart Ambulance System

## Week 1 – Group Formation & Project Setup

During the first week, the group focused on forming a complete team and selecting an appropriate project topic. Initially, there were challenges with group formation due to inactive members and incorrect group allocations.

Several attempts were made to contact all assigned members; however, only a limited number responded. One member confirmed they had been added to the group by mistake, while another expressed willingness to participate. Due to the insufficient number of active members, the issue was raised with the lecturer.

Following this, approval was given to merge with another group experiencing a similar issue. As a result, two smaller groups were combined to form a complete team of four members.

Once the group was finalised, a communication channel was established using Microsoft Teams to facilitate discussions and coordination. The group then discussed potential project ideas and collectively agreed to develop a Smart Ambulance System. This proposal was presented to the lecturer and approved, with the condition that it must meet all assignment requirements.

The group then discussed individual strengths and areas of knowledge, including programming, database management, and system design. Based on this discussion, roles were assigned to each member:

* Patient Monitor System
* Ambulance System
* Hospital System with Login
* Database System

A shared repository was created on GitHub to support collaborative development. The repository was structured with folders such as `src`, `docs`, and `tests`, and initial files were created for each component of the system. A README file was also added to provide an overview of the project.

---

## Week 2 – Initial Development

During Week 2, the group began the development phase of the project. Each member started working on their assigned component, focusing on creating a basic version of their system.

The initial objective was to ensure that each part of the system could run independently without errors. Development included simulating patient data, structuring the ambulance system, preparing the hospital interface, and setting up the database structure.

GitHub was used to track progress, and commits were made regularly to document development activities. Issues were also created to organise tasks and monitor progress.

Some challenges were encountered, particularly in understanding how MQTT communication would be implemented. The group agreed to research this further and integrate it in the following week.

---

## Week 3 – System Integration & Feature Expansion

During Week 3, the group focused on integrating the individual components of the system. MQTT communication was introduced to enable data transmission between the ambulance and hospital systems.

Testing was conducted to ensure that patient data could be successfully sent and received. Several issues were identified during this phase, including connection errors and data formatting inconsistencies.

These issues were addressed through collaborative problem-solving and group discussions. Adjustments were made to improve data handling and ensure compatibility between components.

Additional features were also implemented during this week, including:

* Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA)
* Patient condition tracking
* Recording of treatment or first aid provided
* Preparation for a basic login system

---

## Week 4 – Testing, Debugging & Finalisation

During the final week, the group focused on testing the complete system and resolving any remaining issues. All components were combined and tested together to ensure correct functionality.

The system was tested under different scenarios to verify that:

* Data was correctly transmitted from the ambulance
* The hospital system displayed the information accurately
* The database stored the data reliably

Documentation was reviewed and updated, including the README file and weekly logs. All group members ensured that their contributions were properly committed to the repository.

Final preparations were made for submission, ensuring that all components of the assignment were completed, including the codebase, individual reports, and presentations.

---

## Overall Summary Of The Weeks

Overall, the group demonstrated effective collaboration, communication, and problem-solving throughout the project. Despite initial challenges with group formation, a structured approach and consistent teamwork enabled the successful development of the Smart Ambulance System.
